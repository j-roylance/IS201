The Standard Model

There are 17 known particles in the Standard Model of Particle Physics.
   
They are grouped into fermions and bosons, dependent on spin (which acts like rotation, but nothing is rotating:):
   
  • Fermions (Spin ± 1/2):
    1. Quarks:
      • Generation 1 (lightest mass):
        1. Up (Charge +2/3)
        2. Down (Charge -1/3)
      • Generation 2 (medium mass):
        1. Charm (Charge +2/3)
        2. Strange (Charge -1/3)
      • Generation 3 (greatest mass):
        1. Top (Charge +2/3)
        2. Bottom (Charge -1/3)
    2. Leptons:
      • Generation 1 (lightest mass):
        1. Electron Neutrino (Charge 0)
        2. Electron (Charge -1)
      • Generation 2 (medium mass):
        1. Muon Neutrino (Charge 0)
        2. Tau (Charge -1)
      • Generation 3 (greatest mass):
        1. Tau Neutrino (Charge 0)
        2. Lepton (Charge -1)
  • Bosons (Spin ± Integer)
    • Guage bosons are force carrying particles for 3 fundamental forces (minus gravity because it is annoying):
      1. Electromagnetic force (holds every molecule together):
        • Photon (Spin 1 Charge 0 Mass 0)
      2. Strong Nuclear force (holds the atomic nucleus together):
        • Gluon (Spin 1 Charge 0 Mass 0)
      3. Weak Nuclear force (causes radioactive decay):
        • Z Boson (Spin 1 Charge 0 Mass 91.2 Gigaelectronvolts)
        • W Boson (Spin 1 Charge 0 Mass 80.4 Gigaelectronvolts)
    • Non-Guage Boson - this boson does not transmit force, but merely is an excitation of its field
      1. Higgs force (gives subatomic particles mass [not atoms]):
        • Higgs Boson (Spin 0 Charge 0 Mass 124.97 Gigaelectronvolts)
